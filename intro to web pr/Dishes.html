Dishes

Welcome to our Dishes page, where we invite you on a gastronomic journey through the vibrant and diverse flavors of Lebanese cuisine. Immerse yourself in a rich tapestry of dishes that showcase the unique blend of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern influences. From traditional favorites to modern interpretations, each dish tells a story of heritage and culinary artistry.

Breakfast Dishes

Manousheh:

Manousheh is a traditional Lebanese flatbread topped with various ingredients. The most popular topping is a mixture of za'atar (a blend of thyme, sumac, sesame seeds, and salt) with olive oil.It is typically baked until crispy .

Foul Medames:

Foul Medames is a hearty breakfast dish made from cooked fava beans seasoned with olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, and various spices. It is often garnished with fresh parsley, tomatoes, and radishes. Foul is commonly served with flatbread or pita.

Labneh with Olive Oil and Za'atar:

Labneh is a strained yogurt with a thick, creamy consistency similar to cream cheese. For breakfast, labneh is often spread on a plate, drizzled with high-quality olive oil, and sprinkled with za'atar. It is served with fresh Lebanese bread or pita.

Lunch Dishes

Falafel:

Falafel are deep-fried balls or patties made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices. They are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Falafel is commonly served in pita bread with tahini sauce, tomatoes, and cucumbers.

Fattoush:

Fattoush is a refreshing Lebanese salad that features mixed greens, tomatoes, cucumbers, radishes, and green peppers. It is often garnished with crispy pieces of toasted or fried Lebanese flatbread. The dressing typically includes olive oil, lemon juice, and sumac, giving it a tangy flavor.

Makloubeh:

Makloubeh, meaning "upside-down" in Arabic, is a one-pot dish featuring layers of rice, seasoned meat (chicken, lamb, or beef), and a variety of vegetables such as eggplant, cauliflower, and carrots. After cooking, the dish is flipped upside-down for serving, presenting a beautiful and flavorful arrangement..

Dinner Dishes

Kebbeh

Kebbeh is a traditional Lebanese dish made with a mixture of finely ground meat (often lamb or beef), bulgur wheat, and spices. The mixture is formed into patties or balls and can be baked, fried, or cooked in a variety of ways.

Shawarma

Shawarma is a popular Lebanese street food made by stacking seasoned slices of marinated meat (often chicken, beef, or lamb) on a vertical rotisserie. The meat is slowly cooked as it turns on the spit. Thin slices of the cooked outer layer are shaved off and usually served in flatbreads or wraps.

Mujadara

Mujadara is a comforting and hearty Lebanese dish made with a combination of lentils and rice, flavored with aromatic spices, and topped with caramelized onions. This dish is a nutritious and budget-friendly option.
